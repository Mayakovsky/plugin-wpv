# Whitepaper Grey — Brand & Naming Document

## The Name

**Whitepaper Grey** operates on two levels simultaneously:
the company and its flagship verification agent,
and the human-facing liaison agent, **Grey**.

---

## Company & Product: Whitepaper Grey

### Surface meaning
The grey zone between legitimate and fraudulent —
the ambiguous territory that every DeFi whitepaper occupies
until verified.

### Technical meaning
**Grey literature** is an established term in research and
information science referring to documents produced outside
traditional academic or commercial publishing channels.
Whitepapers are grey literature by definition.
The name is therefore not metaphor — it is classification.
Whitepaper Grey specializes in exactly the document category
its name describes.

### The tension
"Whitepaper" signals technical authority and formality.
"Grey" subverts it with ambiguity and forensic neutrality.
That tension is the product: professional rigor applied to
documents that exist outside formal verification systems.

---

## The Agent: Grey

**Grey** is the liaison between human users and the
Whitepaper Grey verification infrastructure — its services,
agents, and data.

### Why Grey works as an agent name
- Neutral. Not adversarial, not credulous.
- Implies the ability to operate in ambiguous territory
  without losing judgment
- Forensic connotation — grey is the color of analysis,
  not of alarm
- Single word. Clean. Memorable. Works across all
  human-to-agent interaction contexts
